Religious Tourism - Sacred Journey

Holiest Site

Masjid al-Haram (The Grand Mosque)

The world's largest mosque surrounding the Kaaba in Makkah. The holiest site in Islam, welcoming millions of pilgrims annually for Hajj and Umrah with capacity for 4 million worshippers.

House of Allah

The Holy Kaaba

The most sacred structure in Islam, toward which Muslims worldwide face during prayer. Experience the spiritual journey of Tawaf, circumambulating this ancient cubic structure.

Prophet's Mosque

Al-Masjid an-Nabawi (The Prophet's Mosque)

The second holiest mosque in Islam in Madinah, built by Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). Houses the Prophet's tomb and features the iconic Green Dome, welcoming 1 million visitors daily.

Day of Arafat

Mount Arafat (Jabal ar-Rahmah)

The granite hill where pilgrims gather during Hajj on the 9th day of Dhul Hijjah. Known as the Mount of Mercy, where Prophet Muhammad delivered his Farewell Sermon.

Sacred Water

Zamzam Well

The blessed well within Masjid al-Haram providing sacred water to pilgrims. Believed to have miraculously sprung forth for Prophet Ismail and his mother Hajar 4,000 years ago.

Revelation Site

Cave of Hira (Ghar Hira)

The mountain cave on Jabal an-Nour where Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) received the first revelation of the Quran from Angel Jibril (Gabriel), marking the beginning of Islam.

First Mosque

Quba Mosque

The first mosque in Islamic history, built by Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) upon his arrival in Madinah. Praying here is said to equal the reward of an Umrah.

Historic Cemetery

Jannat al-Baqi

The main cemetery of Madinah where many companions of the Prophet and members of his family are buried. A place of profound historical and spiritual significance.

Hajj Ritual

Mina - The City of Tents

The valley where pilgrims spend three days during Hajj, performing the ritual of stoning the Jamarat pillars. Home to the world's largest tent city accommodating 3 million pilgrims.
