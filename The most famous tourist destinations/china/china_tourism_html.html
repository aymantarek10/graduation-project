Must-Visit Attractions

UNESCO World Heritage

Great Wall of China

One of the most iconic structures in human history. Trek along ancient ramparts with breathtaking mountain scenery. A must-see marvel of engineering and civilization.

Imperial Palace

Forbidden City

The largest palace complex in the world with nearly 1,000 buildings. Explore 500 years of Chinese imperial history in this architectural masterpiece.

Ancient Wonder

Terracotta Warriors

Over 8,000 life-sized clay soldiers guarding an ancient emperor's tomb. A remarkable archaeological discovery frozen in time for over 2,000 years.

Modern Marvel

The Bund, Shanghai

Iconic riverside promenade showcasing colonial architecture and modern skyscrapers. Walk along the Huangpu River watching the city's transformation.

Natural Beauty

Li River - Guilin

A UNESCO scenic site featuring limestone karsts and bamboo rafts. Experience traditional Chinese landscape painting come to life in spectacular fashion.

Imperial Garden

Summer Palace, Beijing

A vast ensemble of lakes, gardens, and palaces. The world's largest imperial garden with stunning Kunming Lake views and classical Chinese architecture.

Scenic Wonder

Zhangjiajie National Forest

Towering sandstone pillars rising from mist-covered canyons. The inspiration for Avatar's floating mountains, offering spectacular hiking and cable car rides.

Poetic Landscape

West Lake, Hangzhou

One of China's most scenic locations with ancient temples and traditional gardens. A serene retreat inspiring artists and poets for over a thousand years.

Religious Monument

Leshan Great Buddha

A UNESCO site featuring the world's tallest stone Buddha carved into a cliff. This 71-meter statue overlooks three rivers with incredible spiritual significance.

Culinary Excellence

MICHELIN Star

Jing

One of Beijing's most prestigious restaurants. Modern takes on traditional Peking cuisine with artistic plating and exceptional service at The Peninsula.

Cantonese Cuisine

Yanyuewan

Award-winning traditional dim sum restaurant in Guangzhou. Experience authentic Cantonese dim sum trolleys and classic preparations honoring culinary heritage.

Spicy Sichuan

Lan

Shanghai's premier Sichuan cuisine destination. Bold flavors, numbing Sichuan peppercorns, and authentic dishes showcasing this region's fiery culinary tradition.

Roasted Specialty

Quanjude

Beijing's most famous Peking duck restaurant operating since 1864. Perfected recipe with crispy skin, tender meat, and traditional serving rituals.

Interactive Dining

Xiabu Xiabu

China's most beloved hot pot chain. Cook your own ingredients in aromatic broths with friends and family in this interactive, social dining experience.

Noodle House

Bao Gu Noodles

Celebrated Xi'an noodle restaurant serving hand-pulled ramen and biangbiang noodles. Watch skilled chefs craft noodles before your eyes with traditional techniques.
