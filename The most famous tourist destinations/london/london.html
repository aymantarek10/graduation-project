Iconic Landmarks

London Icon

Big Ben & Houses of Parliament

The iconic clock tower standing 96 meters tall, officially known as Elizabeth Tower. A UNESCO World Heritage Site and symbol of London. Recently restored to its original Victorian glory, Big Ben's chimes have marked time since 1859.

Royal Residence

Buckingham Palace

The official residence of the British monarch with 775 rooms. Witness the spectacular Changing of the Guard ceremony. The State Rooms are open to visitors during summer, showcasing royal treasures and magnificent art collections.

World-Class Museum

Natural History Museum

Home to over 80 million specimens spanning billions of years. Marvel at the iconic dinosaur skeletons, the massive blue whale, and the stunning Romanesque architecture. Free admission makes it perfect for all visitors.

Victorian Marvel

Tower Bridge

London's most famous bridge, built in 1894. Walk across the high-level glass floor walkway for thrilling views of the Thames below. Visit the Victorian Engine Rooms to discover the bridge's fascinating engineering.

1000 Years of History

Tower of London

A historic fortress housing the Crown Jewels and nearly 1000 years of British history. Meet the famous Beefeater guards and ravens. Explore the medieval palace, royal armories, and tales of intrigue and execution.

World Treasures

The British Museum

Over 8 million artifacts spanning 2 million years of human history. Home to the Rosetta Stone, Egyptian mummies, and the Parthenon sculptures. One of the world's greatest museums with free admission.

Sky-High Views

The London Eye

Europe's tallest cantilevered observation wheel at 135 meters. Each rotation takes 30 minutes, offering breathtaking 360-degree views across London. See up to 40 kilometers on a clear day, perfect for sunset visits.

Royal Church

Westminster Abbey

A UNESCO World Heritage Site where kings and queens have been crowned since 1066. Gothic architectural masterpiece and final resting place of monarchs, scientists, and poets including Newton and Dickens.

Royal Park

Hyde Park

London's largest royal park covering 350 acres in the heart of the city. Enjoy the Serpentine lake, Speaker's Corner, beautiful gardens, and the Princess Diana Memorial Fountain. Perfect for picnics and relaxation.

Luxury Hotels

London Legend

The Savoy

An iconic luxury hotel on the Strand since 1889. The first luxury hotel in Britain with electric lights and lifts. Features Art Deco style, the legendary American Bar, and views over the Thames River.

Art Deco Elegance

Claridge's

The epitome of British elegance in Mayfair since 1856. Famous for Art Deco interiors and afternoon tea. A favorite of royalty and celebrities, offering timeless luxury and impeccable service.

Royal Opulence

The Ritz London

Overlooking Green Park since 1906. The definition of elegance with Louis XVI décor and gold leaf embellishments. Famous for traditional afternoon tea and the Michelin-starred Ritz Restaurant.

Mayfair Masterpiece

The Connaught

A sophisticated retreat in Mayfair blending traditional charm with contemporary design. Features two Michelin-starred restaurants and one of London's finest cocktail bars, the Connaught Bar.

Sky-High Luxury

Shangri-La at The Shard

London's highest hotel occupying floors 34-52 of The Shard. Every room offers spectacular panoramic views. Features an infinity pool with stunning city vistas and contemporary Asian-inspired luxury.

Grand Edwardian

Rosewood London

Housed in a beautifully restored Edwardian building in Covent Garden. Features a stunning courtyard entrance, the Scarfes Bar, and Holborn Dining Room serving classic British cuisine.

World-Class Dining

3 MICHELIN Stars

Restaurant Gordon Ramsay

Gordon Ramsay's flagship restaurant in Chelsea, maintaining three Michelin stars since 2001. Exceptional French cuisine with seasonal British ingredients in an elegant, intimate setting.

Artistic Dining

Sketch

A unique gastronomic destination in Mayfair. The Gallery room features pink velvet and contemporary art, while Lecture Room holds two Michelin stars. Instagram-famous for its egg-shaped bathrooms.

Bombay Café

Dishoom

Paying homage to the old Irani cafés of Bombay. Exceptional Indian cuisine in beautiful vintage-inspired settings. Famous for bacon naan rolls at breakfast and House Black Daal. Multiple locations across London.

2 MICHELIN Stars

The Ledbury

Chef Brett Graham's acclaimed restaurant in Notting Hill. Modern European cuisine with British and Australian influences. Known for innovative dishes and exceptional wine pairings.

Best Steakhouse

Hawksmoor

London's finest steakhouse serving ethically-sourced British beef. Dry-aged steaks cooked to perfection, legendary Sunday roasts, and exceptional cocktails. Multiple locations including the stunning Air Street restaurant.

1 MICHELIN Star

Gymkhana

Contemporary Indian cuisine in a setting inspired by colonial Indian gymkhana clubs. Michelin-starred restaurant offering refined regional dishes with game meats and seafood specialties.

Grand Café

The Wolseley

A grand European café-restaurant on Piccadilly. Housed in a former car showroom with stunning 1920s architecture. Serves breakfast through dinner with classic dishes and impeccable service.

24-Hour Dining

Duck & Waffle

London's highest 24-hour restaurant on the 40th floor. Stunning city views and innovative British cuisine. The signature duck and waffle dish is legendary, perfect for late-night dining or sunrise breakfast.

Since 1798

Rules

London's oldest restaurant in Covent Garden. Specializing in traditional British game and classic dishes. Rich history with Edward VII's private dining room and walls adorned with theatrical memorabilia.
