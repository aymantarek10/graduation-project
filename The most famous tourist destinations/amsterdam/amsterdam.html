Must-Visit Attractions

UNESCO Heritage

Amsterdam Canal Ring

The iconic 17th-century canal system with 165 waterways and 1,281 bridges. A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring stunning gabled houses and perfect for boat tours.

Art Masterpiece

Van Gogh Museum

Home to the world's largest collection of Van Gogh's works, including The Sunflowers and The Bedroom. Over 200 paintings, 500 drawings, and 750 letters from the legendary artist.

City Heart

Dam Square

Amsterdam's main square and bustling heart, featuring the Royal Palace, National Monument, and Nieuwe Kerk. A vibrant hub for events, street performers, and city exploration.

Historic Site

Anne Frank House

The poignant museum where Anne Frank wrote her famous diary during WWII. Walk through the secret annex and learn about her life and the Holocaust's impact.

Dutch Masters

Rijksmuseum

The Netherlands' premier art museum featuring Rembrandt's Night Watch, Vermeer's Milkmaid, and 8,000 objects of Dutch Golden Age art and history across 80 galleries.

Charming Quarter

Jordaan District

Amsterdam's most charming neighborhood with narrow streets, independent art galleries, cozy cafes, vintage shops, and the famous Monday flea market at Noordermarkt.

Urban Oasis

Vondelpark

Amsterdam's largest and most popular park spanning 120 acres. Perfect for cycling, picnicking, outdoor concerts, and people-watching. Features ponds, playgrounds, and the iconic pavilion.

Skyline Views

A'DAM Lookout

Observation deck on the 20th floor offering 360-degree panoramic views of Amsterdam. Features Europe's highest swing, interactive exhibits, and a revolving restaurant.

Floating Market

Bloemenmarkt

The world's only floating flower market on the Singel canal. Browse vibrant tulips, bulbs, and Dutch souvenirs from traditional barges dating back to 1862.

Iconic Canal Hotels

Canal Palace

Waldorf Astoria Amsterdam

Six 17th-century canal houses transformed into a luxurious hotel on the prestigious Herengracht. Experience royal elegance with Michelin-starred dining and spa facilities.

Historic Charm

Pulitzer Amsterdam

A collection of 25 interconnected Golden Age canal houses along the Prinsengracht and Keizersgracht. Boutique luxury blending historic architecture with contemporary design.

Grand Heritage

Hotel de l'Europe

Amsterdam's most prestigious hotel since 1896, overlooking the Amstel River. Classic European luxury with stunning views of the Munt Tower and historic city center.

Boutique Luxury

The Dylan Amsterdam

A former 17th-century theater transformed into an intimate boutique hotel on the Keizersgracht. Michelin-starred restaurant and uniquely designed suites blend East-meets-West aesthetics.

Modern Classic

Conservatorium Hotel

Housed in a restored bank building near Museumplein. Contemporary five-star luxury with a world-class spa, multiple dining venues, and proximity to major museums.

Creative Design

Andaz Amsterdam Prinsengracht

Designed by Marcel Wanders in a former public library. Bold, artistic interiors with canal views, rooftop garden, and a vibrant atmosphere in the heart of the city.

World-Class Dining

3 Michelin Stars

Ciel Bleu

Amsterdam's only two-Michelin-starred restaurant on the 23rd floor of Hotel Okura. Contemporary French cuisine with breathtaking panoramic city views.

Michelin Star

Restaurant Vermeer

One-Michelin-starred dining at the NH Barbizon Palace. Modern Dutch cuisine using seasonal local ingredients in an elegant canal-side setting.

Grand Cafe

The Duchess

Stunning Art Deco restaurant in a former bank vault. European cuisine with a focus on sharing plates, oysters, and craft cocktails in an opulent atmosphere.

Modern European

Breda

Casual fine dining in a former blacksmith's workshop. Creative European dishes with an open kitchen, intimate atmosphere, and focus on sustainable ingredients.

Farm-to-Table

De Kas

Unique greenhouse restaurant serving ingredients grown on-site and from local farms. Daily changing menu featuring the freshest seasonal produce in a stunning glass structure.

Authentic Japanese

Yamazato

One-Michelin-starred Japanese restaurant at Hotel Okura. Traditional kaiseki cuisine with teppanyaki, sushi, and tempura prepared by master chefs from Japan.

Dutch Heritage

Greetje

Authentic Dutch cuisine reimagined with modern techniques. Traditional recipes from all provinces of the Netherlands served in a cozy, historic canal house setting.

Fine Dining

Bord'Eau

Two-Michelin-starred restaurant at Hotel de l'Europe. Innovative French cuisine with surprising flavor combinations and impeccable service overlooking the Amstel River.

Historic Setting

Vinkeles

One-Michelin-starred restaurant in an 18th-century bakery at The Dylan. Contemporary French cuisine in an intimate candlelit dining room with exposed brick walls.
